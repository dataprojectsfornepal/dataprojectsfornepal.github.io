Evidence for better policy

Nepal’s historic transition to federalism has surged the demand for more and better data. There is a need to transform existing data into actionable insights and policy outcomes that improve people’s lives, and hold others accountable. To achieve this, Nepalis – across all the sectors – need to access and use timely and actionable data to make decisions, enhance public accountability, identify risks and opportunities, track progress, and more. Bearing this in mind, the World Bank is working to propel the data sharing and using capacity of wider population, and in the end, optimize the value of data.



Capacity Building

Nepal Data Literacy Program

Nepal Data Literacy Program is a 100-hour data literacy curriculum designed by the World Bank team to catalyze stronger data driven decision-making by government and non-government actors. As part of the program, a 100 hour training of the trainers (T-O-T) workshop was delivered in 2019. The course materials that covers topics such as spreadsheets, python, tableau etc. to find, analyze, visualize and synthesize data has also been curated in an open data portal.

Geo Node

GeoNode, a web-based, open source software that enables users to access, share, and visualize geospatial data. It aims to solve to a set of common, overlapping problems shared by a number of different organizations.

Data in News

The OD4B team developed an indicator model to assess the use and engagement of statistics in Nepali news portals along with an accompanying dashboard visualizes the results of the analysis. The dashboard will keep track of the use of data and statistics in six news portals, as it automatically adds more articles to its analysis dataset on a daily basis.

Measuring inequality of Access modeling

This paper provides descriptive analyses of accessibility trends to services at national, provincial, municipal, and geographic scales and suggests research possibilities unlocked by a general purpose model.

Nepal Development Update

Nepal Development Update is World Bank Nepal's biannual publication that reports on key economic developments over the preceding months and examines topics of particular policy significance. The special focus for December 2019 update, was on "Envisioning a future data ecosystem in a federal Nepal"

Open Data for Business Report

Open Data for Business report presents learnings from our exploratory learning exercise to assess the use of data in banking, education and media sectors in Nepal. This report is intended to help inform a long-term strategy to increase the business use of government data in Nepal.
